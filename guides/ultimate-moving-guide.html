Moving Guide

Ultimate Moving Guide

A practical, week-by-week plan for a move in the Bradenton and Sarasota area.

Get a free quote Call (941) 809-5777

Most moving stress comes from doing everything in the last 48 hours. This guide spreads the work across the weeks before your move so the day itself is boring, which is exactly what you want a moving day to be.

It is written for the Bradenton and Sarasota area specifically, so it accounts for things like condo move-in windows, summer heat, and hurricane-season timing.

Six to eight weeks out

Get written estimates from licensed movers. Start a folder for receipts and your inventory. Use up the freezer and pantry so you move less food. If you are leaving a rental, check the notice your lease requires.

Four weeks out

Order boxes and supplies, or have your mover bring them. Begin packing the rooms you barely use: guest rooms, closets, the garage. Label every box by room and a one-line list of contents. Confirm your move date and any building elevator reservation.

Two weeks out

File your change of address with USPS. Transfer or set up utilities at the new place. Pack everything except daily essentials. Confirm crew size and arrival window with your mover.

Moving week

Pack a first-night box: medications, chargers, a change of clothes, basic toiletries, coffee. Keep valuables and documents with you, not on the truck. Defrost the fridge. Take photos of electronics before unplugging so you can reconnect them fast.

Moving day

Do a walkthrough with the crew lead before they start. Point out fragile and do-not-move items. Keep the path clear and the AC on. Do a final walkthrough together before the truck leaves so nothing is missed.

Frequently asked questions

When is the cheapest time to move in the Sarasota area?expand_more

Late spring through summer is usually quieter and easier to book than the winter snowbird season, though the heat means earlier start times.

How early should I book a mover?expand_more

Two to three weeks in season, about a week off season. Earlier is always safer for a weekend date.
